What is my gym routine?

Strength training builds muscle, power, and confidence. My split as of now is Push, Pull, Legs, Upper, Lower. With this split I am able to efficiently hit every muscle group 2x a week.

Every set that I do, I do 2x times till failure with 1-2 warmup sets before.

Core principles

PrincipleWhat it means
Progressive overloadAdd more weight each set, try to increase 2.5 lbs every week
Form firstFull ROM, stable bracing, controlled tempo
RecoverySleep, protein, deloads keep gains moving

Who am I?

I am just a guy who wasn't happy with how he looked. People who go to the gym are either going through something, wanting to look better, or both. It's a wonderful place I found in highschool that just lets you escape reality and better yourself at the same time.

I’m about steady progress, not ego lifts. Strong today, stronger next month.

When I train

On Mondays, Wednesdays, and Fridays I go from 10AM-12PM, on Tuesdays and Thursdays I go from 5PM-7PM. This is what works best with my class schedule

  • Push: Incline, shoulders, triceps
  • Pull: Lats, rows, pullovers, biceps
  • Legs: Quads, hamstrings, calves
  • Upper: Mix of push and pull
  • Lower: Squat and deadlift focus

Where I like to lift

I typically workout at the UREC. I don't have a reason to pay for a membership anywhere else when I have one on campus. It's overall pretty good too.

Wipe equipment, re-rack plates, give spotters a heads-up. Respect the space.

Gym checklist

  • Many squat racks and benches
  • Plenty of plates and dumbbells up to 100+
  • Chalk-friendly or towels allowed

How I train

  1. Warm up: 5–8 min cardio, mobility, specific ramp sets
  2. Top set: heavy but clean, then backoff volume
  3. Accessories: target weak links and posture
  4. Log: sets, reps, RPE, notes for next time
